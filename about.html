The SEO Guru

Don Phelps.
The Man Behind NSYGHT.

Founder & The SEO Guru  |  Austin, Texas
Military Veteran 20+ Years in SEO Podcast Host NSYGHT Founder Family Man

Don Phelps does not like to talk about himself. If you ask him about his accomplishments he will redirect the conversation to what he is working on for a client, what he learned from a recent campaign, or how he thinks the search landscape is shifting. That quality, the instinct to put others first, runs through everything he does professionally and personally.

It also happens to be what makes him very good at what he does. People who are genuinely motivated by wanting others to succeed, and who are honest when things do not go the way they planned, tend to build the kind of trust that sustains long-term client relationships. At NSYGHT, most clients stay. That does not happen by accident.

The Story

Don grew up a standout athlete in baseball and football through high school and into the military. Sports taught him the same things the military later reinforced: that preparation matters more than talent, that teams win when everyone carries their weight, and that the gap between good and great is almost always effort and discipline.

He served for several years, including deployments overseas. Military service sharpened the qualities he had already: the discipline, the attention to detail, the ability to stay calm when the situation is complicated, and the commitment to seeing things through regardless of how uncomfortable it gets. He brought all of that back home and eventually pointed it at search engine optimization.

The Military Mindset

Don approaches SEO the way he approached service: proactively, systematically, and with full accountability. When something does not perform, he is the first to acknowledge it and the first to figure out why. When the search landscape shifts, he has usually been studying it for months before most agencies even notice the change.

Twenty Years of Living and Breathing SEO

Don has been doing search engine optimization for over 20 years. That span covers more algorithm changes, platform shifts, and industry disruptions than most practitioners in the field have seen. He was doing local SEO before most people knew what local SEO was. He was building Google Business Profiles when the platform was still called Google Places.

What has kept him ahead is not luck. It is the habit of constant learning that he has maintained since the beginning. Countless late nights reading research, testing theories, studying case studies, and pushing to understand not just what is working now but why it is working and what that implies about where things are going next. That habit has not changed. If anything it has intensified as the search landscape has grown more complex with the rise of AI-generated search answers and the shift toward multi-surface visibility.

That continuous learning feeds directly back into client work. When Don understands a new development in the search algorithm or a shift in how AI systems are selecting content to cite, that knowledge gets applied to active campaigns. Clients benefit from an operator who is genuinely invested in the craft, not just executing a fixed playbook.

Continuous Learning as a Core Practice

Don still spends significant time every week studying the evolving search landscape. Not because he has to. Because the industry moves fast and his clients depend on him staying ahead of it. That commitment to ongoing learning is what separates a practitioner who is genuinely current from one who is executing strategies that were relevant three years ago.

NSYGHT: What It Is and Why It Exists

Don founded NSYGHT about nine years ago with a clear purpose: to give local service businesses access to the kind of search strategy and execution that was previously only available to larger companies with bigger budgets. The business he wanted to build was one where clients were treated like partners, where results were reported honestly, and where the work was done with the same discipline and attention to detail he brought to everything else in his life.

The NSYGHT approach reflects Don's character directly. Month-to-month engagements because clients should stay because the work is performing, not because they are locked in. Honest assessments because telling a client what they want to hear is a short-term strategy that always ends badly. No percentage of ad spend because the incentive structure should align with client outcomes, not agency revenue. And when things do not go the way they should, Don is the first person to acknowledge it and the first to do something about it.

Those values also show up in how the NSYGHT search strategy is structured. The four-layer stack of SEO, GEO, AEO, and AIO is not a product. It is a framework that reflects how search actually works in 2026, where visibility across traditional results, AI-generated answers, and position zero placements all matter and all require different but complementary strategies.

When something does not perform the way it should, Don is the first to say so and the first to figure out why. No spin, no blame-shifting, no hiding behind complexity. Just an honest look at what happened and what to do about it.

Family and Home

Don is married to a wonderful woman from Mexico and has a beautiful family. His family is central to who he is and why he works the way he does. The same loyalty and commitment that defined his military service and his career are what he brings to the people he loves. Austin has been home, and the community there matters to him.

He does not broadcast any of this. It is not his style. But it is worth saying because the values that make someone a good partner, a good parent, and a good friend are the same ones that make someone trustworthy in business. Don is genuinely one of the good ones.
